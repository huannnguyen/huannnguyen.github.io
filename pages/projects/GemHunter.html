    Gem Hunter

    Community-Driven Discovery App

    Gem Hunter helps people find hidden, local experiences by combining personalized recommendations, community contributions, and a gamified reputation system designed in an HCI course.

    üîó Link to the project page


    The Problem

    Existing discovery platforms often show generic, overcrowded, or impersonal recommendations. People want experiences that feel unique, local, and personally relevant‚Äîbut these can be difficult to find without digging through many sources.

    Our Solution

    We designed a mobile app where:

    • Users discover hidden gems through a location-based map.
    • They can add their own gems, photos, and tips.
    • Reputation points reward exploration and contributions.
    • A customizable radius + filter system supports spontaneous discovery.
    • Accessibility-focused alt-text ensures inclusive use.

    Process (High-Level)

    • User Research: Surveys with locals and visitors revealed that people often rely on word-of-mouth and spontaneous exploration rather than big platforms.
    • Prototyping: Paper sketches ‚Üí low-fi mockups ‚Üí high-fi Figma screens.
    • Usability Testing: Led to adding a rating system, clearer navigation, and in-app feedback pop-ups.
    • Iteration: Simplified UI, improved clarity, and removed unnecessary friction.

    Key Learnings

    • People prefer flexible, spontaneous discovery rather than strict itineraries.
    • Ratings and clear navigation greatly improve trust and usability.
    • In-app feedback is more seamless than push notifications.
    • Accessibility requires intentional decisions at every stage‚Äîespecially alt-text.

    Impact

    Gem Hunter blends gamification, community sharing, and accessible design to help users meaningfully explore their surroundings. It encourages discovery that is personal, rewarding, and culturally diverse.
