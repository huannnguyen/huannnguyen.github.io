    CSE 440

    Introduction to HCI: User Interface Design, Prototyping, and Evaluation

    HCI theory and methods for designing, prototyping, and evaluating user interfaces, with a focus on human factors, technology, and usability tools.

    This course introduced me to the core theories and methods of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), with a strong focus on designing, prototyping, and evaluating user interfaces. Instead of focusing on development, the class emphasized the full design process‚Äîfrom identifying a problem to creating and iterating on digital prototypes. I worked with a team to ideate, research user needs, explore design opportunities, and test our solutions through peer and instructor feedback. Throughout the quarter, I gained hands-on experience with interaction design, design research methods, scenario development, storyboarding, and rapid prototyping. The course helped me develop a deeper understanding of usability, human factors, and how to critically evaluate the impact of design decisions.
